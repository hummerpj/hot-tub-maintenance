# Water Balance Cheat Sheet
## 2019 Master Spas Twilight 8.2 — 380 gallons

---

## Target Numbers (with ClearBlue Ionizer + Ozone)

| Parameter | Target | Action if Off |
|---|---|---|
| **Free Chlorine (FC)** | 1–2 ppm | Add bleach; shock to 8 ppm after heavy use |
| **pH** | 7.2–7.8 | Low: add baking soda / High: add dry acid |
| **Total Alkalinity (TA)** | 50 ppm | Too high: add acid + aerate (pH/TA dance) |
| **Calcium Hardness (CH)** | 120–150 ppm | Low: add calcium chloride |
| **CYA** | 20–30 ppm | Set at startup with dichlor or trichlor; switch to bleach |
| **Borates** | 50 ppm | Top up with boric acid if below 30 ppm |
| **MgCl₂** | 200–300 ppm | Add once at startup (therapeutic / silky feel) |
| **TDS** | < 1,500 ppm above baseline | If exceeded: drain and refill |

> **Why 1–2 ppm FC?** The ClearBlue ionizer deposits copper and silver ions that handle bacteria and algae continuously. The ozonator oxidizes organic contaminants on contact. Together they do most of the heavy lifting — chlorine only needs to serve as a residual safety net. Keeping FC at 1–2 ppm avoids the harshness and smell of 3–5 ppm while still passing the "dip a test strip and see a reading" sanity check.

---

## Treatment Systems Installed

- **ClearBlue Mineral System** — continuously releases copper/silver ions; handles primary bacterial and algae control
- **Lamudo Ozone Generator SPA-124** — destroys contaminants via oxidation; dramatically reduces sanitizer consumption
- **Ozonator output connector:** OZONE terminal on Balboa BP501/601 board

**What each system does for you:**

| System | Primary Role | What It Replaces |
|---|---|---|
| ClearBlue ionizer | Kills bacteria & algae (copper/silver) | Most of the chlorine's sanitizing work |
| Ozonator | Oxidizes organics, oils, lotions | Shock oxidation between soaks |
| Chlorine (residual) | Backup sanitizer + measurable safety net | Nothing — still needed, just at lower levels |

## Startup / Fresh Fill — Do This in Order

1. **Purge plumbing** with AhhSome biofilm cleaner, run jets 20 min, drain, wipe shell
2. Fill through filter compartment (prevents air lock)
3. **Sanitize first** — add dichlor to reach 5 ppm FC (~5 tsp for 380 gal)
4. **Adjust pH to 7.2–7.4** using dry acid (~1–2 tsp drops pH by ~0.2)
5. **Lower TA to 50 ppm** — add acid to pH 7.0–7.2, then aerate; repeat over a few days
6. **Adjust CH to 120–150 ppm** — add calcium chloride pre-dissolved in bucket
7. **Build CYA to 20–30 ppm** using either method below, then switch to bleach permanently
8. **Add boric acid** — ~15 oz (430g / 1¾ cups) dissolved in warm tub water
9. **Add MgCl₂ flakes** — 2–3 cups (460–690g) food-grade flakes dissolved in warm water
10. **Verify ClearBlue and ozonator are running** — confirm blue indicator light on ionizer; check ozone bubbles at jet

## CYA Buildup Tracker — Trichlor Granules

Trichlor granules (90% available chlorine) are the most concentrated stabilized chlorine. In 380 gallons:

- **1 tsp (~5 g)** → raises FC ~3 ppm, adds ~2 ppm CYA
- **1 tbsp (~15 g)** → raises FC ~9 ppm, adds ~6 ppm CYA

The chlorine gets consumed, but the CYA stays permanently — it only leaves when you drain.

### CYA Tracking Table (teaspoon doses)

| Tsp Doses (cumulative) | Trichlor Added | Approx CYA | FC per Dose | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0 g | 0 ppm | — | Fresh fill |
| 1 | 5 g | ~2 ppm | +3 ppm | Keep going |
| 3 | 15 g | ~6 ppm | +3 ppm | Keep going |
| 5 | 25 g | ~10 ppm | +3 ppm | Keep going |
| 8 | 40 g | ~16 ppm | +3 ppm | Getting close — test |
| 10 | 50 g | ~20 ppm | +3 ppm | In range (low end) — test to confirm |
| 13 | 65 g | ~26 ppm | +3 ppm | In range |
| 15 | 75 g | ~30 ppm | +3 ppm | At target — **stop trichlor, switch to bleach** |

**How to use this table:** Track cumulative teaspoon doses. Each dose bumps FC by ~3 ppm, which is a reasonable spike that'll settle back down. Test CYA with your kit after you're about halfway to your target — real-world results vary with splash-out, water additions, and granule density.

### Dichlor Alternative (if you prefer granules)

Each ½ tsp (2.7 g) dose of dichlor raises FC ~1 ppm and adds ~0.9 ppm CYA. To reach 30 ppm CYA you'd need roughly **33 daily doses** of ½ tsp — about a month of daily dosing.

| Doses (½ tsp each) | Approx CYA Level |
|---|---|
| 10 | ~9 ppm |
| 20 | ~18 ppm |
| 25 | ~23 ppm |
| 30 | ~27 ppm |
| 33 | ~30 ppm — **switch to bleach** |

## Dosing Quick-Reference (380 gallons)

| Chemical | Purpose | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Trichlor granules (90%) | Raise FC 3 ppm (+2 ppm CYA) | 1 tsp (5 g) |
| Dichlor (56%) | Raise FC 1 ppm (+0.9 ppm CYA) | ½ tsp (2.7 g) |
| Bleach 6% | Raise FC 1 ppm | 5 tsp (0.85 fl oz) |
| Bleach 8.25% | Raise FC 1 ppm | 3.5 tsp (0.62 fl oz) |
| Bleach 10% | Raise FC 1 ppm | 3 tsp (0.5 fl oz) |
| Dry acid | Lower TA 10 ppm / pH ~0.2 | 1 oz (28 g) |
| Muriatic acid 31% | Lower TA 10 ppm | 0.8 fl oz (24 mL) |
| Baking soda | Raise TA 10 ppm | 1.5 tbsp (28 g) |
| Calcium chloride | Raise CH 10 ppm | 1.5 tbsp (23 g) |
| Boric acid (99%) | Top up borates 10 ppm | ⅓ cup (86 g) |

**Post-soak bleach rule:** With ionizer + ozone running, dose ~2–3 tsp of 6% bleach per person-hour at 104°F. Two people, 30 min soak ≈ 2–3 tsp. The ozone will continue oxidizing contaminants after you close the cover — the bleach bump just restores your measurable FC residual to the 1–2 ppm target.

## Routine Schedule

### Daily (60 seconds)
- Test FC and pH with strips or drop kit
- Add bleach if FC is below 1 ppm (usually just a splash — 1 to 2 tsp)
- Dose extra bleach after soaking (see post-soak rule above)

### Weekly
- Shock to 8 ppm FC (crack cover 15–20 min to off-gas)
- Test TA
- Quick rinse of filter with garden hose

### Monthly
- Test CH, CYA, borates, TDS
- Deep-clean/soak filter cartridge in filter cleaner solution
- Top up borates if below 30 ppm
- Check TDS meter — drain if > 1,500 ppm above baseline
- Inspect ClearBlue electrode — clean mineral buildup with white vinegar if needed

### Every 3–4 Months (drain & refill)
- Run AhhSome purge before draining
- Refill and rebalance from Step 1 above
- Drain sooner if TDS spikes past 1,500 ppm above baseline
- Formula: *(380 gal ÷ 3) ÷ avg daily bathers = days between drains*

## Practical Tips

### Chlorine & Sanitizing
- **"Just a splash" is usually enough.** With ionizer + ozone doing the real work, your daily bleach addition is tiny — often less than a tablespoon. If you're dumping lots of bleach daily, something else is off (pH too high, biofilm, failing ozone, etc.).
- **Don't chase perfect numbers.** FC of 0.5 ppm with ionizer + ozone running is safer than 5 ppm chlorine-only. The test strip just needs to show *something* — you're checking that the residual safety net exists, not relying on chlorine as your primary sanitizer.
- **Shock is still important.** Even with ozone, a weekly shock to 8 ppm clears anything that accumulated. Do it the night before a rest day so it has time to drop back to 1–2 ppm.

### CYA Management
- **Once CYA hits your target, stop all stabilized chlorine.** No more trichlor tabs, no more dichlor. Bleach only from that point forward. CYA doesn't break down — it only leaves when water leaves.
- **Test CYA before adding more tabs.** Overshoot is annoying — the only fix is dilution (partial drain and refill).
- **If CYA creeps above 50 ppm**, chlorine becomes sluggish. With your low FC target of 1–2 ppm, high CYA is especially bad — plan a partial drain.
- **After a drain-and-refill, CYA resets to 0.** You'll need to rebuild it with trichlor or dichlor again.

### Water Feel & Comfort
- **Borates make the water feel silky** and act as a pH buffer. Once added, they only leave through splash-out — you'll rarely need to top up more than a couple tablespoons per month.
- **MgCl₂ is therapeutic** (muscle relaxation) but raises TDS. Monitor TDS monthly — if it climbs past 1,500 ppm above your fresh-fill baseline, it's time to drain.
- **pH drift is normal.** The ozonator and aeration from jets push pH up over time. A pinch of dry acid every few days is expected, not a problem.

### Equipment & Maintenance
- **ClearBlue electrode life:** Typically 6–12 months depending on output setting. If you notice the ionizer light behaving differently or water clarity declining, inspect the electrode.
- **Ozone check:** Occasionally look for micro-bubbles at the ozone jet while the pump runs. No bubbles = ozone may not be generating. The Lamudo unit lasts roughly 3–5 years before the ozone cell weakens.
- **Filter is your first line of defense.** A clogged filter makes everything else harder. Rinse weekly, soak monthly, replace annually (or when pleats stay discolored after soaking).
- **Keep a spare filter cartridge** so you can rotate — soak one while the other runs.

### Testing & Tools
- **TF-100 test kit** (Taylor reagents, large bottles) is the recommended kit for accuracy
- **Borate strips** (Taylor or LaMotte) for monthly borate checks
- **TDS meter** ($10–15) is essential — track TDS monthly with MgCl₂ in the water
- **PoolMath app** (TroubleFreePool.com) — enter 380 gallons for exact custom doses
- **Test before adding chemicals, not after.** Add chemicals, wait 20 min with jets on, then test to confirm — don't dose again immediately.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Always use food-grade MgCl₂** — agricultural grade contains iron that turns water yellow/green
- **Borates don't degrade** — only lost through splash-out; test monthly, not weekly
- With ClearBlue + Ozone: expect noticeably lower bleach consumption vs. chlorine-only — a gallon of bleach may last you a month or more
- **Never mix chemicals before adding to tub** — add one at a time, dissolve separately if needed
- **Run jets for 15–20 min after adding any chemical** to ensure full circulation and mixing
